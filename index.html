The Gita is presented as a conversation between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. The dialogue takes place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, just before the start of a great war.

The teachings of the Gita cover a wide range of topics, including duty (dharma) , righteousness, the nature of the self (atman), the paths of devotion (bhakti), knowledge (jnana), and selfless action (karma).
